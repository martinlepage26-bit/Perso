Astral Glossary

Breath of the Astral Year reads the gaialogy as a grounded language for earthly life. This glossary names what each sign is for, how it feels in the body, how it moves through relations, and where it can fray.

Browse the signs Open the Astral app Back to the book page

Use this page as a reading key. The sign language here is descriptive rather than predictive, and each entry stays close to the book's vocabulary.

How to read it

Competence

The sign's practical talent, the kind of movement it knows how to make well.

Civic weather

The public atmosphere the sign names: rupture, reciprocity, structure, release, and similar climates.

Body and relation

Body register, social pattern, shadow, and ritual tell you how the sign behaves when lived, strained, or carried forward.

Reading guide

The glossary works as a translation layer.

It turns each sign into a compact set of definitions that future seasonal reading output can use without becoming vague or generic.

1. Competence

Start with what the sign does well. That is the sign's most useful motion, not its marketing line.

2. Weather

The civic theme and body register show the sign's climate in public life and in lived sensation.

3. Pressure

Shadow and ritual keep the language honest. They mark where the sign bends badly and how it can be used again without flattening into cliché.

The signs

Twelve entries, grouped by element.

Fire, earth, air, and water set the larger climate. Each sign then gives that climate a more exact voice.

Why it matters

This is the book's vocabulary bank.

The glossary keeps the project from collapsing into loose seasonal reading prose. It gives each sign a stable profile so future pages can generate interpretations that still sound like Breath of the Astral Year.

Use case

Reference, then reuse.

Read the sign once as a glossary entry. Use it again as a source for seasonal reading copy, app copy, or any later Astral interface that needs the language to stay grounded.
