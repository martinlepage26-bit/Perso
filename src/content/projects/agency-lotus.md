*Agency LOTUS* belongs on the site because it shows a more operational side of the practice: not just interpretation or governance language, but working desktop tooling for handling live research material.

Inside the repository, LOTUS is presented as a standalone note-scoring workspace that accepts Markdown and text uploads, scores them across strategic and meaning-oriented dimensions, and shares a common data root with *Dr. Sort-Academic Helper*. That makes it useful as both a discrete application and part of a broader archive workflow.

The right framing for now is modest and concrete. This is a local-first prototype with clear functionality, a defined role inside the repository, and a public GitHub link for people who want to inspect the build directly.
